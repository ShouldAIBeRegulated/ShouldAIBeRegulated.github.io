Tim Cook

Tim Cook is the CEO of Apple, but despite the fact that his company is adding more and more AI into its codebase by the week, he refuses to even explore that future with a reporter. In essence, Cook states that it’s not that he doesn’t know the direction his company is taking in terms of AI. He instead claims that he does not like that direction, and he’d simply rather not discuss it.

Although this seems like a weak and worrying statement for the CEO of one of the world’s most cutting edge AI companies to make, it certainly holds some weight.

In other words, Cook is not doubtful in his analysis. He knows the direction of his company completely. What he is uncomfortable about exploring with the press is the clearly muddled implications of what is to come out of his company in the future.

Optimally, he wishes that the company will never get there, so that he does not have to grapple with those issues himself. He feels that the current pace of AI development is simply inappropriate, as we have not truly understood AI yet.

Cook is a man who prides his company on delivering features that they believe in. AI is a feature that he believes is, at its core, morally, ethically, and even capably ambiguous. He doesn’t know what should be the limit of its functionality and its access, as he believes we simply shouldn’t have to deal with something so potentially dangerous given our current circumstances.

Most importantly, Cook states that he is not comfortable with full scale automation and AI deployment. He states that when ‘technological advancement can go up exponentially, I think there’s a risk of losing sight that tech should serve humanity, not the other way around.” Clearly, Cook is thinking of the true implications of deploying powerful AI.

One thing is for sure right now- Tim Cook, and as a result, his company, is not keen on exploring the future of ever-increasing AI development as a technology to integrate into products.

Stephen Hawking

Among those who believed that AI will spiral out of control is the late renowned professor Stephen Hawking. Prof. Hawking says that the technology in AI we have may be quite useful now, but his fears will truly culminate when AI surpasses the power of humans.

He states the possible existence of a superintelligence, claiming that such an intelligence would be able to iterate upon itself and build itself to become even better. In other words, this software would be so intelligent that it would be able to update itself.

Hawking believes that this point will be the point where we as a race will confront a massive issue.

Experts claim that we are still quite a ways off from achieving the compute power to develop an AI that is a true artificial intelligence, but they can’t ultimately know whether the result will be detrimental, beneficial, or indifferent to humans.

Hawking believes that the future could go in one of two directions. Once the development of AI reaches a point where it can perform all the tasks that a human needs to in order to survive, it will be faced with a couple of options.

If the wealth created by the presence of all these machines is distributed among the people, everyone will benefit and enjoy lives of plenty. However, if the wealth is controlled and not redistributed, we will experience poverty and disparity the likes of which we have never seen before.

He claims that, in his opinion, the world is currently leaning towards the latter option. In that sense, Hawking drops his worrisome viewpoint that we are indeed veering towards a very dangerous path with the development of AI. If anything, the restructuring of human psychology and society has to precede such an astronomical development in AI, or we will be finished for sure. Essentially, Hawking claims that we are going down the wrong path socio-politically, and that in turn is why our foray into powerful general intelligence will lead us to our doom. He ultimately advocates for the idea that we in our current state should not face this issue.
