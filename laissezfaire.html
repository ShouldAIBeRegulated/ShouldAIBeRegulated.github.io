No, laissez-faire is the way to go.

Innovation will outweigh the detriments!

AI is a technology that should be allowed to grow and flourish as it does. Although some may see certain detriments that come with the ever-increasing pace of AI-tech development, the benefits that come along with it far outweigh the costs, figuratively and literally.

Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft

Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella is one of the people at the forefront of AI development. He states that AI can make the world far more inclusive, citing the fact that there are billions of people in the world who are not partaking in societies or economies. He goes on to claim that the rise of AI can allow such people to have opportunities not previously presented to them. He states that such tasks as this, which are difficult for humans to direct manpower and time towards, are much more suited for optimization by artificial intelligence. Humans on this planet will certainly need the help of AI to make sure everybody lives on an even playing field.

Overall, from eliminating bias in decisions usually skewed by human bias to even eventually being able to communicate with his son, who has cerebral palsy, Nadella chooses to focus on the overwhelming benefit that comes with AI. He states that it is a foundational challenge of AI to create tools that humans can constrain, but he stands behind his corporation’s method of making sure their work is okay; the developers set their own standards. This leads to a fairly quick developmental model, without any government limitation on what is being created. Ultimately, Nadella believes innovation like this will propel the world into a new generation of prosperity, and the best way to get there is to simply let AI development run its course and trust that work will be done responsibly. As the CEO of a large tech company, it’s his job to lead this revolution, so he is determined that his approach is the correct one. We can expect Microsoft and its employees to ultimately ‘follow the leader’ and adopt this point of view.

TechCrunch: Technology Enthusiasts

From the perspective of people who’ve been using tech for a long time, AI is nothing short of a modern marvel. When you’ve come to appreciate technology, along with its nuances, strengths and weaknesses, like the writers at TechCrunch have, you develop a new respect for the true capabilities of AI. That being said, TechCrunch writers are championing the development of AI. They speak of the capabilities of AI as if it will change the world, and they also mention how the benefits will clearly outweigh the possible issues that AI development presents.

techcrunch office picture

Ray Kurzweil: Heavily Involved Computer Scientist

Kurzweil looks at the clear benefits. In his TIME article, he focuses on what would be important to the layman reading the paper. As he has experience as a computer scientist himself being very involved in the AI behind optical character recognition, his ideas are well educated and supported by his clear experience in the field. He mentions all the benefits of AI technology. In this way his argument is not one which refutes the counterarguments- he simply ignores them. He claims that the benefits are so astronomical, and that we will truly become a better race because of them. Due to this alone, his argument that AI should not be fear stands. Unlike Nadella, he goes deeper into actually exploring some restrictions upon AI development, but those seem to be afterthoughts in the face of his true treatment of AI.
