Yes, Let's Restrict AI Development.

Limitations need to be established.

Kate Crawford

Kate Crawford is a Microsoft Research Scholar who is well respected in the tech industry. She is also a Distinguished research professor at New York University, and her work regarding machine learning is unparalleled. As a person who is well ingrained in the field of Artificial Intelligence research, she has some strong opinions of her own. In particular, she’s worried about how much we will actually believe the answers an unbounded AI will give us.

The professor claims that AI cannot truly be free of bias. She states that we are at a moment in history where AI development and AI produced results do not imply actual truth. This itself could be detrimental to human society if it were determining anything substantial, such as involvement in a crime, or likelihood to even commit crime again.

Whether it’s helping governmental regimes achieve their own ends, whatever they may be, or helping companies in the private sector take advantage of existing societal structures, she believes AI can operate without clear restriction in many ways. She brings to light many such creations, a very notable example being when President Trump tried to persuade data company Palantir to create a Muslim registry using AI.

She states that it’s best to be skeptical about anything that is ‘free of bias’, as it was originally trained with human data. She states that AI can be used to these ends, and it might be slightly worrying given the implications- decision making power in the hands of a biased entity is a scary thing.

In other words, we really can’t trust AI technology in the way that it operates right now- the restrictions around it are just simply not stringent enough. Crawford’s argument is that we simply need to establish more bounds and regulation for this tech, to improve the ethics in its use, as well as the accuracy of the software itself.

She closes with some remarks that carry a sense of futility; she reminds us that AI technology will inevitably be used to ends such as the ones she has described. Nevertheless, she emphasizes that we should remain wary and prepare ourselves for a future in which AI opinion might be held at a much higher regard than it is now. This means we will effectively be controlled by such technologies, whether they have the proper restrictions and boundaries set upon them or not. She ends the interview with an ominous warning: “Done right, AI can be used to keep power structures in check. But in the wrong hands…”

Elon Musk

One cannot go a day in the tech industry without reading a news article about famous business magnate Elon Musk. Needless to say, he’s got some pretty interesting opinions regarding AI. Here are his two cents on the issue.

Elon musk seems to view AI development with a general air of caution. Not only is he at the forefront of AI development as the head of Tesla in its effort to create self-driving cars, he is also a bastion of the tech industry in general, holding key chunks of companies that are all the centerpieces of the tech industry. In other words, Musk’s knowledge and expertise is nothing short of first class in the world of tech.

Musk says that the solution is to take a more proactive approach. He states that we should be proactive instead of reactive in the field of AI, as we have to take special care to set constraints upon what we develop. He states his distaste for the current way of handling things, which he claims is when the government ‘steps in after too many bad things happen’. In other words, he believes current policy regarding regulation only makes the government step in when it will be too late.

Aside from his pop culture references, however, we cannot forget that Musk is working on one of the most advanced AI technologies of our time, Tesla Autopilot, which will eventually face plenty of moral conflicts. An example of these would be if an AI had to decide who to kill in a crash- a young or old person.

Furthermore, Musk likes to engage in behaviors where he keeps a watchful eye over AI development. He has admitted to holding stakes in AI companies simply to ‘take a look’ at what was being developed, so that he himself could be prepared to see the changes in AI industry. This goes to show how wary he was of AI development as a whole.

Musk may be at the cutting edge of AI technology as we know it, but he seems to be very fearful of it as a whole and advocates for a future in which we impose very stringent restrictions on AI itself. If not, he warns, we could be in a lot of trouble.
