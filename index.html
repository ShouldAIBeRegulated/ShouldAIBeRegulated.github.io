Should AI development be regulated?

Compiled by Akilesh Praveen

What's AI All About?

AI as a concept, although fascinating, is not truly essential to this debate. AI itself is the innate ability of a computer to make decisions it is not explicitly told to make, based on its own knowledge. Over the years, computer scientists have found various ways to create this phenomenon within computer programs. However, it is not the mere concept of AI, but the applications of it that sits at the root of this debate. In recent years, AI technology has become so developed and available that it’s being leveraged for a multitude of purposes. As these purposes grow to encompass more and more parts of everyday life, it becomes increasingly more important to examine the implications of these formidable technologies.

AI In Our Time

Now, more than ever, we are witness to the lightning-fast pace of AI technology advancement. Facial recognition technology doesn’t just help us unlock our phones, it allows us to be spied on in public and spoofed into videos.

source: Gaurav Oberoi, KDNuggets.com

Not only does Machine learning help us identify the emotional value in a text message, but it also helps companies optimize sales by determining what to sell us and at what price. Self-driving cars do not just let us adjust our hair while we’re driving, it may have to decide between killing an old man and a child on the street.

Common psychological problems that humans have grappled with for years, the basis of our very own morals to this date, are just now being explored by artificial intelligence. A great example of this relates to the issue of Tesla’s self driving cars deciding between two people to kill in a car accident, given they have an equal opportunity to kill one of two people, but not save both. For an example of such a problem, take a moment to check out the infamous 'trolley problem' which became an internet phenomenon in early 2018.

source: trolleydillema.com , FIND SOURCE

As artificial intelligence, powered by industry leaders such as Google, Facebook, and Apple, ingrains itself further into our lives, we are forced to consider it from many perspectives aside from just technological advancement alone. It is simply not enough to ignore the obvious ethical and moral implications that come with such powerful technology anymore.

A Response Is Needed

The truth is, AI is developing faster than we as a species ever imagined. The fact that we ourselves cannot accurately predict what will transpire as a result of this technology is a fairly sizable red flag in and of itself. That being said, action needs to be taken. Many ways of dealing with these changes have been proposed by a multitude of people, ranging from high-profile tech CEOs, to University Professors, to Tech Journalists to the engineers that built this technology themselves.

Overall, opinions are divided. As of now, there seems to be no dominant, prominent voice urging us to move towards true laissez-faire development of AI-tech or a locked-down heavily scrutinized development model.

Despite the fact that there is so much disagreement on this subject, technology nevertheless continues to advance. As the renowned Sam Harris, a neuroscientist, researcher and philosopher, would say:

PICTURE OF HARRIS

“Fifty years is not that much time to meet one of the greatest challenges our species will ever face”

What Will It Be?

The decision is yours to make. So, what’s it going to be? Take a look at these stances and decide for yourself.

Should we restrict AI development? Or maybe let development continue without restrictions at all? Or should we just never get there in the first place?
